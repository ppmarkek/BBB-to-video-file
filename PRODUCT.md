# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A student working on a Windows PC, usually immediately after receiving a lecture recording. They need one reliable place to find a lecture, begin a new one, and eventually turn it into a structured study note without losing track of their materials.

## Product Purpose

Turn lecture recordings and related materials into useful, well-organised study lessons. The first interaction must make the next action obvious: find an existing lecture or start a new one.

## Brand Personality

Calm, strict, modern. The interface should feel like a focused study workspace: clear priorities, little visual noise, and confident feedback.

## Anti-references

Avoid playful education-game motifs, loud gradients, excessive dashboards, and generic AI chat surfaces. Do not use crowded card grids or decorative animation that makes the task slower.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next learning action unmistakable.
2. Let the library stay quiet until it contains useful material.
3. Prefer clear states and source context over decorative metrics.
4. Keep personal study materials visibly local and under the user's control.
5. Use motion only to explain a transition or confirm feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use high-contrast text, a readable system typeface, keyboard-operable controls, and respect Windows reduced-motion preferences where available. Animation must remain short and never conceal content.
